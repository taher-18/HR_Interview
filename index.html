Interviewer Cheatsheet

How to read the ratings

★
1 — Off. Real concern, wouldn't be a fit right now.
★★
2 — Weak. Answer felt thin or off, small yellow flag.
★★★
3 — Solid. Fine answer, didn't blow you away or worry you.
★★★★
4 — Great. Genuinely impressed, you'd happily work with them.
★★★★★
5 — Wow. Memorable, the kind you immediately want on the team.

How to actually run it

  • Make them comfortable first. Smile, small talk for 1 min, then start.
  • It's a chat, not a quiz. Skip questions if they already answered them naturally.
  • Use the follow-up prompts. They're there to keep the convo flowing — don't read them like a script.
  • Let silence breathe. Wait 3 seconds before jumping in. That's where the real answer lives.
  • "Tell me more" is your most powerful line.
  • Take quick notes, score right after each question while it's fresh.
  • Watch the vibe. Are they warm? Closed off? Curious? It matters as much as the answers.

Green flags ✅

  • Specific stories with their own role clearly named.
  • Self-aware — can name a real weakness without spinning it.
  • Honest about how much time they can actually give.
  • Stays calm and thoughtful on situational questions.
  • Asks something real about the club at the end.
  • Talks about past teammates with respect, even when describing problems.
  • Mentions small things that show they care about people (checking in, noticing quiet members).

Red flags 🚩

  • Blames everyone but themselves in past team stories.
  • "Whenever you need me" with no real number on availability.
  • Everything is generic — no examples, ever.
  • Gets visibly defensive about feedback or criticism.
  • Trash-talks past teams, friends, or other clubs.
  • Only talks about CV, certificates, recognition — no real motivation.
  • Zero curiosity at the end ("no questions").
  • Says all the "right" answers but it sounds rehearsed and empty.

Yellow flags 🟡 (worth probing)

  • Strong answers but very short — push for examples.
  • Mentions being "very busy" multiple times.
  • Avoids conflict-related questions or rushes through them.
  • Names friends already in the club a lot — fit might be social only.

Final verdict — keep it human

  • 85–100 · Strong Yes: Take them, don't overthink it.
  • 70–84 · Yes: Solid fit, would add to the team.
  • 50–69 · Maybe: Talk it out with the team — could go either way.
  • Below 50 · No: Not the right fit right now.

The score is a guide, not a verdict. Trust your gut on fit — if everyone says "yes" but no one's excited, it's probably a Maybe.
