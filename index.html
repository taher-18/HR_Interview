Interviewer Cheatsheet

Rating scale

★
1 — Poor. Major concerns, doesn't meet bar.
★★
2 — Below average. Some red flags or weak answer.
★★★
3 — Acceptable. Meets bar, no standout.
★★★★
4 — Strong. Above expectations, clear thinking.
★★★★★
5 — Exceptional. Stands out, would actively want them.

Live interview tips

  • Listen more than you talk. Aim for 70/30 candidate vs you.
  • Use silence. Wait 3 seconds after they finish — best info comes here.
  • Probe with "why". "Tell me more" beats moving on.
  • Watch for vague answers. Ask for specific examples.
  • Take quick notes. Score after the question, not during.

Red flags 🚩

  • Blames others for past conflicts.
  • Vague on time commitment.
  • Generic answers, no examples.
  • Defensive when asked about criticism.
  • Speaks negatively about past teams.

Green flags ✅

  • Self-aware about weaknesses.
  • Specific stories with their actual role.
  • Honest about time they can give.
  • Asks thoughtful questions back.
  • Calm under stress questions.

Verdict thresholds

  • 85–100: Strong Yes
  • 70–84: Yes
  • 50–69: Maybe — discuss with team
  • Below 50: No
